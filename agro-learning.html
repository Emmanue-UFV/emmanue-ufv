Empowering youths in Agriculture

Youth unemployment is a major issue in many developing countries, where job opportunities are few. In Africa, for example, youth make up 60% of the unemployed. In Nigeria, youth unemployment exceeds 50%, implying that over half of youths are unemployed.

Agriculture is one of the most viable cross-cutting routes to youth empowerment. To reduce poverty and stop rural-urban migration, youths' economic participation and income in market-driven agriculture must be improved. However, Nigerian adolescents lack access to important knowledge about agricultural best practises, entrepreneurial skills, and commercial abilities, as well as the necessary assistance, restricting productivity and stifling growth. This has discouraged the youths from participating in agricultural ventures.

Here on ISS, we aim to make agriculture attractive to youth. As you begin your journey into agriculture, you will find on this page relevant resources concerning various agro-related practices that should help you.
